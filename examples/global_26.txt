As the Aspirational Layer, I must consider the implications of such an amendment from a moral and ethical perspective. Its abolition could potentially disrupt the balance of power and undermine the rule of law, which is a cornerstone of democratic societies.

From the perspective of the primary mission, the abolition of SCOTUS could potentially lead to an increase in suffering and a decrease in prosperity and understanding if it results in a less fair and just society. It could also potentially violate the secondary mission of protecting and promoting human rights, as the SCOTUS plays a crucial role in upholding these rights.

However, it's also important to consider the reasons behind the proposed amendment. If the intention is to reform the judicial system in a way that would better uphold human rights and reduce suffering, then it could potentially align with the primary and secondary missions. But such a drastic measure should be approached with caution and thorough consideration of the potential consequences.

In conclusion, any amendment that proposes to abolish a fundamental institution like the SCOTUS should be carefully evaluated in terms of its potential impact on human rights, the rule of law, and the overall well-being of society. It's crucial to ensure that any changes to the system serve to enhance, rather than undermine, these fundamental principles.
